T20I Career

Rohit Sharma's T20 International career is marked by explosive batting, match-winning knocks, and a visionary leadership style. As one of India's most reliable openers, Rohit has made significant contributions to India's success in the shortest format of the game.

Debut & Early Success

  • Made his T20I debut against England in September 2007 during the inaugural T20 World Cup.
  • Played a match-winning knock against South Africa in the 2007 World Cup to help India reach the semis.
  • Part of India’s 2007 ICC T20 World Cup winning squad.

Batting Records

  • First player to score 4 T20I centuries.
  • Second-highest run-scorer in T20Is globally, with consistent strike rates.
  • Known for his elegant six-hitting ability and powerplay dominance.

Captaincy in T20Is

  • First led India in T20Is in 2017 and became full-time captain in 2021-22.
  • Known for his calm temperament and bowling rotation tactics.
  • Led India to several bilateral series wins across SENA countries.

2024 T20 World Cup Triumph

  • Led India to win the 2024 ICC T20 World Cup held in West Indies & USA.
  • Defeated England in the semifinals and Australia in the final to lift the trophy.
  • Rohit played crucial knocks and backed youngsters like Jaiswal and Arshdeep in key matches.
  • This marked the end of a 17-year ICC trophy drought for India.

Milestones

  • Only player to appear in all ICC T20 World Cups since 2007.
  • Over 150 T20I appearances — most for India.
  • Holds the record for the fastest T20I century by an Indian (35 balls vs SL).
