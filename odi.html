ODI Career

Rohit Sharma's journey in One Day Internationals (ODIs) is nothing short of phenomenal. From a young and talented batsman struggling for consistency, he has transformed into one of the greatest ODI openers in cricket history.

Debut & Rise (2007-2012)

  • Debuted in June 2007 vs Ireland.
  • Early promise seen during the 2007-08 CB series in Australia, including a match-winning 66 in the final.
  • Struggled with form and consistency initially, leading to occasional drops from the squad.

Transformation as an Opener (2013 Onwards)

  • Promoted to opener in 2013 Champions Trophy, a career-defining moment.
  • Scored 177 runs in the tournament, helping India lift the trophy.
  • Since then, became a permanent fixture and formed a formidable opening partnership with Shikhar Dhawan.

Double Centuries

  • 264 vs Sri Lanka in 2014 — the highest individual score in ODI history.
  • 209 vs Australia in 2013 — his first double ton.
  • 208* vs Sri Lanka in 2017 — as captain.

World Cup Performances

  • 2015 World Cup: 330 runs including a century vs Bangladesh in the quarterfinal.
  • 2019 World Cup: 5 centuries, the most by any player in a single edition.
  • Scored 648 runs in 2019, finishing as the highest run scorer.

2025 ICC Champions Trophy Victory

  • Rohit Sharma captained India to clinch the prestigious 2025 ICC Champions Trophy.
  • Played vital innings throughout the tournament, showcasing brilliant leadership and batting.
  • Instrumental in the final match with a composed 85 runs under pressure.
  • This triumph added a major feather to his illustrious ODI career as captain.

Captaincy in ODIs

  • Led India to Asia Cup 2018 title in absence of Virat Kohli.
  • Full-time ODI captain since 2022.
  • Known for strategic field settings and calm demeanor.

Records & Achievements

  • Fastest century by an Indian in ODIs — off 35 balls vs Sri Lanka (2017).
  • Most ODI double centuries (3).
  • Among top 5 all-time run scorers for India in ODIs.
