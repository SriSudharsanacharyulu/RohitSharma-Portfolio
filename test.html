Test Career

Rohit Sharma’s journey in Test cricket was not as immediate as in limited overs, but once he found his rhythm, he became a reliable force, especially on home soil.

Debut & Early Years

  • Made his Test debut in November 2013 vs West Indies, scoring 177 runs in his very first innings.
  • Followed it up with another century (111*) in his second match.
  • Initially played in the middle-order but struggled with form and consistency overseas.

Transformation as Opener

  • In 2019, was promoted to opener against South Africa and responded with 529 runs in the series, including two centuries and a double century.
  • Scored his first Test double hundred — 212 in Ranchi.
  • His form at the top helped India dominate in home conditions.

Overseas Success

  • Scored a gritty 161 vs England in Chennai, 2021 — rated as one of his best innings.
  • Played a pivotal role in India’s historic win at Lord’s during the 2021 England tour.
  • Registered his first overseas century — 127 at The Oval in the same series.

Captaincy & Current Role

  • Became India's Test captain in early 2022.
  • Led India to the WTC Final 2023.
  • Known for bringing composure and tactical depth as a leader in the red-ball format.

Stats & Milestones

  • 3000+ runs in Test cricket.
  • Average above 45 as an opener in home conditions.
  • 7 centuries and 1 double century.
