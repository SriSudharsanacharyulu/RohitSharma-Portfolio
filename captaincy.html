Captaincy Highlights

Rohit Sharma's captaincy has been a transformative phase in Indian cricket, marked by tactical brilliance, calm leadership, and significant trophies.

Initial Captaincy Roles

  • Appointed vice-captain for limited-overs formats in 2017.
  • Led India in Asia Cup 2018 during Virat Kohli’s absence and secured the title.
  • Demonstrated strategic acumen and composure in high-pressure matches.

Full-time Captaincy (2022 Onwards)

  • Officially took over as full-time captain in all formats from 2022.
  • Known for flexible and aggressive captaincy, often changing field placements to outsmart opponents.
  • Maintains a calm and composed demeanor, earning respect from teammates and critics alike.

Major Trophies as Captain

  • 2023 ICC World Test Championship — led India to a historic victory.
  • 2024 T20 World Cup — captained the team to a thrilling tournament win.
  • 2025 ICC Champions Trophy — secured the title with a balanced team effort.
  • Asia Cup titles and other bilateral series wins, highlighting consistency and winning mentality.

Captaincy Style & Strengths

  • Highly intuitive captain, adapting strategies according to pitch conditions and opposition strengths.
  • Excellent man-management skills fostering team harmony and confidence.
  • Emphasis on backing young talent and creating a fearless team environment.
  • Recognized for cool-headed decisions during crunch moments.

Records & Milestones as Captain

  • Most wins as captain in T20 internationals for India.
  • Highest win percentage among Indian captains in ODIs and Tests (minimum 20 matches).
  • First Indian captain to win all three ICC trophies.
