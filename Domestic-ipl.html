Domestic & IPL Career

Early Domestic Career

Rohit Sharma started his domestic cricket journey playing for Mumbai in the Ranji Trophy, where his solid performances quickly marked him as a rising star in Indian cricket.

  • Made first-class debut for Mumbai in 2006.
  • Played pivotal roles in Mumbai’s Ranji Trophy wins.
  • Known for calm technique and ability to build innings under pressure.

Indian Premier League (IPL) Journey

Rohit's IPL career is legendary, especially with Mumbai Indians (MI), where he grew from a talented batsman into one of the best T20 captains globally.

  • Started IPL career with Deccan Chargers in 2008.
  • Joined Mumbai Indians in 2011 and became captain in 2013.
  • Led MI to a record 5 IPL titles (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2020).
  • IPL records include highest individual score of 264 runs (also a world record in List A cricket).
  • Most IPL centuries by any player.
  • Known for calm leadership, tactical acumen, and finishing games under pressure.

Notable IPL Highlights

  • Captaincy debut in 2013 leading MI to their maiden IPL title.
  • 5 IPL titles as captain — a record in IPL history.
  • Multiple ‘Man of the Match’ awards for match-winning knocks.
  • Instrumental in mentoring young Indian players and building a strong team culture.

Records & Achievements in Domestic & IPL

  • Fastest 50 in IPL history (off 15 balls) in 2013.
  • Highest run scorer for Mumbai Indians across IPL seasons.
  • Ranji Trophy winner multiple times with Mumbai.
